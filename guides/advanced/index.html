    Advanced Guide¶

    Advanced tactics, optimization strategies, and mastery techniques for experienced Hunter: The Reckoning 5th Edition players.

    This guide covers character optimization, Edge synergies, Desperation management, and high-level play.

    Character Optimization¶

    Maximizing Dice Pools¶

    The 8-Dice Threshold: - 8+ dice pools have high success rates - Target: Primary attribute 4 + Primary skill 4 = 8 dice - Secondary pools: 5-7 dice - Tertiary pools: 3-4 dice acceptable

    Optimization path:

    Starting (0 XP): - Dexterity 4, Firearms 4 = 8 dice (shooting) - Intelligence 3, Investigation 3 = 6 dice (clues) - Manipulation 2, Persuasion 2 = 4 dice (social)

    Early (20 XP): - Raise Firearms to 5 = 9 dice shooting (10 XP) - Add 2 specialties (6 XP) - Save 4 XP

    Mid (50 XP): - Raise Dexterity to 5 = 10 dice shooting (20 XP) - Raise Investigation to 4 = 7 dice investigating (8 XP) - Buy second Edge (15 XP) - Remaining: 7 XP

    Attribute Priority¶

    Combat character: 1. Dexterity 4-5 (hit targets) 2. Stamina 3-4 (survive hits) 3. Wits 3 (initiative) 4. Composure/Resolve 2-3 each (Willpower 4-6)

    Social character: 1. Charisma or Manipulation 4-5 (influence) 2. Composure 3-4 (resist pressure) 3. Wits or Intelligence 3 (read situations) 4. Dexterity 2 (minimum combat capability)

    Investigation character: 1. Intelligence 4-5 (analyze) 2. Wits 4 (notice and act fast) 3. Composure 3 (Willpower for mental resistance) 4. Dexterity 3 (basic combat)

    Skill Priority¶

    Essential skills (everyone): - Awareness 3 - Spot threats and clues - Athletics 2 - Dodge and maneuver - One combat skill 3-4 - Survive fights

    Combat specialists: - Firearms 4-5 or Melee 4-5 - Athletics 3 - Dodge - Stealth 2-3 - Positioning

    Social specialists: - Persuasion or Intimidation 4-5 - Insight 3 - Read people - Subterfuge 3 - Deception

    Investigation specialists: - Investigation 4-5 - Occult 3-4 - Awareness 4

    Specialty Placement¶

    Priority order: 1. Primary attack skill (Firearms, Melee, Brawl) 2. Primary investigation skill (Investigation, Occult, Awareness) 3. Primary social skill (Persuasion, Subterfuge, Insight)

    Avoid: - Specialties on skills with only 1 dot - Specialties too broad to apply often - Duplicate specialty types

    Derived Stats Optimization¶

    Health = Stamina + 3: - Minimum: 5 (Stamina 2) - Recommended: 6 (Stamina 3) - Optimal: 7+ (Stamina 4-5)

    Willpower = Composure + Resolve: - Minimum: 4 (2 + 2) - Recommended: 5 (3 + 2 or 2 + 3) - Optimal: 6+ (3 + 3 or 4 + 2)

    Edge Synergies¶

    Combat Synergies¶

    Arsenal + Avenger: - Heavy armament - Vengeance-driven warrior - Always armed and ready - Best skills: Firearms 5, Athletics 3, Intimidation 3

    Fleet + Defender: - Mobile protector - Rapid response - Superior positioning - Best skills: Melee 4, Athletics 4, Awareness 3

    Senses + Any combat Creed: - Never surprised - Enhanced awareness in fights - Spot ambushes - Best skills: Awareness 5, Firearms 4, Investigation 3

    Social Synergies¶

    Contacts + Defender: - Community network - Information hub - Social protection - Best skills: Persuasion 4, Insight 4, Streetwise 3

    Fame + Redeemer: - Public influence - Leverage celebrity - Redemption through visibility - Best skills: Persuasion 5, Performance 4, Leadership 3

    Status + Zealot: - Institutional authority - Command structure - Organized crusade - Best skills: Leadership 4, Intimidation 4, Politics 3

    Investigation Synergies¶

    Library + Innocent: - Research-driven discovery - Knowledge accumulation - Understanding the supernatural - Best skills: Investigation 5, Academics 4, Occult 4

    Third Eye + Any Creed: - See supernatural truth - Identify threats instantly - Mystical perception - Best skills: Awareness 4, Occult 4, Investigation 3

    Global Access + Defender: - Information network - Track threats globally - Protect proactively - Best skills: Investigation 4, Technology 4, Awareness 3

    Support Synergies¶

    Martyr + Defender: - Protect through sacrifice - Tank damage for allies - Ultimate guardian - Best skills: Medicine 4, Athletics 3, Composure 4

    Inspire + Any combat Creed: - Leadership in battle - Buff allies - Force multiplier - Best skills: Leadership 5, Firearms 4, Charisma 4

    Benediction + Redeemer: - Divine healing - Spiritual protection - Redemptive power - Best skills: Medicine 4, Persuasion 3, Composure 4

    Multi-Edge Combos (High XP)¶

    Arsenal + Fleet: - Mobile heavy weapons platform - Hit-and-run tactics - Superior firepower + positioning

    Contacts + Library: - Information dominance - Human and textual sources - Know everything

    Senses + Third Eye: - Ultimate perception - See both mundane and supernatural - Nothing escapes notice

    Inspire + Martyr: - Ultimate support - Buff and protect team - Battlefield leadership

    Desperation Management¶

    When to Use Desperation¶

    Always use when: - Character death is imminent - Ally is dying - Mission-critical roll (one chance) - Final confrontation with major threat

    Usually use when: - Danger is low (0-3) - Success is very important - Failure has severe consequences - Storyteller hints it's needed

    Rarely use when: - Danger is high (7-10) - Roll is not critical - Other options exist - You're already in Despair

    Never use when: - Routine tasks - Low-stakes situations - You can afford to fail - Risk of Despair > reward

    Danger Management¶

    Keep Danger low: - Rest and downtime (-1 per session) - Complete Redemption paths - Avoid unnecessary Desperation use - Ask Storyteller for Danger reduction opportunities

    Monitor thresholds: - 0-3: Safe to use Desperation - 4-6: Caution advised - 7-9: High risk - 10: Extreme danger

    Danger reduction tactics: - Narrative downtime between arcs - Completing personal storylines - Redemption paths - Storyteller rewards

    Overreach Decisions¶

    When you Overreach (Success + Desperation 1s):

    Choose success when: - You can handle the Danger increase - Success is worth the cost - You're at low Danger already - Mission success > personal safety

    Choose Despair when: - Danger is already high (8+) - Failure advances character story - Redemption path is interesting - You want dramatic character moment

    Example: 

    Danger 2, roll 6 successes, 2 Desperation 1s
Choose: Keep 6 successes + gain Danger 2 (total Danger 4)
Reason: Danger 4 is still safe, success is critical

    Despair Strategy¶

    Embrace Despair narratively: - Don't avoid it out of fear - It's a feature, not a punishment - Creates great character moments - Drives personal stories

    Plan Redemption: - Work with Storyteller - Tie to your Drive - Make it meaningful - Use it to advance character arc

    Redemption examples:

    Vengeance Drive in Despair: - Redemption: Hunt down and hurt your quarry - Approach: 1-2 session mini-arc - Completion: Dramatic confrontation

    Virtue Drive in Despair: - Redemption: Uphold morals despite darkness - Approach: Moral dilemma, choose right path - Completion: Resist temptation

    Combat Tactics¶

    Action Economy¶

    Maximize effectiveness per turn: - Attack with highest pool - Position for next turn - Coordinate with allies - Don't waste actions on low-probability moves

    Positioning¶

    Use environment: - Cover: Increases difficulty to hit you - High ground: Bonus to attack - Choke points: Limit enemy movement - Escape routes: Always have one

    Describe tactics: - "I take cover behind the car" = +1 difficulty to hit you - "I flank while they're distracted" = -1 difficulty to hit - "I climb to the roof" = high ground advantage

    Focus Fire¶

    Concentrate attacks: - All Hunters attack one target - Eliminate threats quickly - Reduces enemy action economy - Protects wounded allies

    Priority targets: 1. Healers/support - Eliminate first 2. High damage dealers - Remove threats 3. Weak enemies - Easy kills 4. Leaders - Disrupt coordination

    Desperation in Combat¶

    Critical moments: 

    /roll pool:8 difficulty:4 desperate:true comment:Killing blow on vampire lord

    Don't waste on: - Minions - Early fight rounds - When allies can succeed - If you're at high Danger

    Defensive Tactics¶

    Dodge vs. Tank: - Dodge: Dexterity + Athletics (avoid damage) - Tank: Stamina + Resolve (absorb damage) - High Dex = dodge - High Stamina = tank

    When to retreat: - Health below 3 - Willpower depleted - Allies down - Outnumbered 3-to-1 or more

    Diverse sources: - Physical evidence: Investigation rolls - Witnesses: Social rolls (Persuasion, Intimidation) - Research: Academics, Occult rolls - Networks: Use Contacts Edge - Technology: Technology skill for hacking/research

    Research Tactics¶

    Library Edge optimization: - Focus on obscure knowledge - Leverage +2 dice on research - Cross-reference sources - Find patterns

    Occult research: - Identify creature types - Find weaknesses - Understand rituals - Prevent supernatural events

    Social Manipulation¶

    Reading People¶

    Always open with Insight: 

    /roll pool:5 difficulty:2 comment:Reading their demeanor

    Learn: - Emotional state - Motivations - Hidden agendas - Weaknesses

    Persuasion vs. Intimidation¶

    Use Persuasion when: - Target is neutral or friendly - You need cooperation - Long-term relationship matters - Subtlety is required

    Use Intimidation when: - Target is hostile - Quick compliance needed - Relationship doesn't matter - Show of force is appropriate

    /roll pool:7 difficulty:3 comment:Securing cooperation

    Using Fame and Status¶

    Fame tactics: - Leverage public recognition - Manipulate media - Access exclusive areas - Influence public opinion

    Status tactics: - Exercise authority - Command subordinates - Bypass bureaucracy - Intimidate through position

    Multi-Character Builds¶

    The Specialist¶

    Philosophy: Master one thing completely

    Example: Sniper - Dexterity 5, Firearms 5 = 10 dice - Specialties: Firearms (Rifles), Firearms (Long Range), Awareness (Spotting) - Edge: Arsenal (always have perfect weapon) - Role: Long-range elimination

    Strengths: - Unmatched at primary role - Reliable specialist

    Weaknesses: - Vulnerable outside specialty - Party dependent

    The Generalist¶

    Philosophy: Contribute to everything

    Example: Jack-of-All-Trades - Balanced attributes (3s and 2s) - Many skills at 2-3 - Edge: Scrounger or Fleet - Role: Fill gaps, backup everyone

    Strengths: - Adaptable - Never useless

    Weaknesses: - Never best at anything - Slower progression

    The Hybrid¶

    Philosophy: Excel at two areas

    Example: Combat Investigator - Dexterity 4, Intelligence 4 - Firearms 4, Investigation 4 - Edge: Senses - Role: Investigate, then eliminate

    Strengths: - Versatile in two areas - Valuable in most scenes

    Weaknesses: - Split XP investment - Slower mastery

    The Support¶

    Philosophy: Make others better

    Example: Team Leader - Charisma 5, Composure 4 - Leadership 5, Medicine 3, Inspire Edge - Role: Buff allies, heal, coordinate

    Strengths: - Force multiplier - Team survivability

    Weaknesses: - Low personal power - Depends on team

    Optimization Quick Reference¶

    XP Priorities¶

    0-20 XP: 1. Raise primary skill to 4 (8 XP) 2. Buy 2-3 specialties (6-9 XP) 3. Save remainder

    21-50 XP: 1. Raise primary attribute to 5 (20 XP) 2. Raise primary skill to 5 (10 XP) 3. Buy second Edge (15-20 XP)

    51-100 XP: 1. Raise secondary attributes to 4 (12-16 XP each) 2. Raise secondary skills to 4 (8 XP each) 3. Buy Perks (5-10 XP each)

    Dice Pool Targets¶

    • Primary: 8-10 dice
    • Secondary: 5-7 dice
    • Tertiary: 3-4 dice
    • Minimum: 3 dice (don't roll less)

    Stat Minimums¶

    • Health: 5+ (Stamina 2+)
    • Willpower: 4+ (Composure + Resolve = 4+)
    • Primary attribute: 4 minimum
    • Primary skill: 4 minimum
    • Awareness: 2 minimum
    • Combat skill: 2 minimum
