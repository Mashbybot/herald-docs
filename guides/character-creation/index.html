    Character Creation Guide¶

    A complete guide to building Hunters in Herald for Hunter: The Reckoning 5th Edition.

    This guide covers everything from concept to optimization, with example builds and strategic advice.

    Character Concept¶

    Before entering any Herald commands, develop your character's concept.

    The Three Questions¶

    1. Who are you? - Name and background - Profession before Hunting - Personality and motivations

    2. Why do you Hunt? - What revealed the truth to you? - What keeps you Hunting despite the danger? - What's your ultimate goal?

    3. How do you Hunt? - Combat specialist or support? - Social manipulator or investigator? - Lone wolf or team player?

    Concept Examples¶

    The Detective - Former police officer who discovered vampires - Hunts to protect the innocent - Investigative and tactical approach

    The Priest - Catholic priest who witnessed demonic possession - Hunts to redeem the corrupted - Spiritual and compassionate approach

    The Soldier - Special forces veteran who lost their squad to werewolves - Hunts for vengeance - Direct combat approach

    The Student - College student who researched the wrong occult tome - Hunts out of curiosity and responsibility - Research and knowledge-based approach

    The Doctor - ER surgeon who treated supernatural injuries - Hunts to save lives - Medical and support approach

    Attribute Allocation¶

    You distribute 9 dots across 9 attributes (all start at 1).

    Distribution Patterns¶

    Specialist (7/4/2): - One category at 4 dots (7 total after base 1s) - One category at 3 dots (6 total) - One category at 2 dots (5 total) - Best for focused characters

    Generalist (5/5/3): - Two categories at 4 dots each - One category at 1 dot - Best for versatile characters

    Balanced (5/4/4): - Distributed more evenly - No major weaknesses - Best for uncertain roles

    Attribute Categories¶

    Physical (Strength, Dexterity, Stamina): - Strength - Melee damage, intimidation, athletics - Dexterity - Firearms, driving, stealth, initiative - Stamina - Health, endurance, resisting damage

    Social (Charisma, Manipulation, Composure): - Charisma - Leadership, persuasion, first impressions - Manipulation - Deception, subterfuge, coercion - Composure - Willpower, staying calm, resisting social pressure

    Mental (Intelligence, Wits, Resolve): - Intelligence - Research, investigation, knowledge - Wits - Awareness, quick thinking, initiative - Resolve - Willpower, determination, mental resistance

    Strategic Priorities¶

    Combat Character: - Dexterity 3-4 (firearms accuracy) - Stamina 3-4 (survive longer) - Wits 2-3 (better initiative)

    Social Character: - Charisma or Manipulation 3-4 (influence) - Composure 3-4 (resist pressure) - Intelligence 2-3 (read situations)

    Investigation Character: - Intelligence 3-4 (analyze clues) - Wits 3-4 (notice details) - Composure 2-3 (stay focused)

    Skill Selection¶

    You distribute 11 dots across 27 skills (all start at 0).

    Distribution Patterns¶

    Specialist (7/4/0): - 7 dots in primary category - 4 dots in secondary category - 0 dots in tertiary category - Best for focused roles

    Balanced (5/4/2) or (4/4/3): - More even distribution - Fewer weaknesses - Best for versatile characters

    Skill Categories¶

    Physical Skills: - Athletics - Running, climbing, dodging, jumping - Brawl - Unarmed combat, grappling - Craft - Building, repairing, creating - Driving - Vehicles, chases, maneuvering - Firearms - Guns, ranged combat - Larceny - Lockpicking, security systems - Melee - Armed close combat - Stealth - Hiding, sneaking, ambushing - Survival - Tracking, outdoors, first aid

    Social Skills: - Animal Ken - Understanding and handling animals - Etiquette - Social graces, protocol - Insight - Reading people, detecting lies - Intimidation - Threats, fear, coercion - Leadership - Inspiring, commanding, organizing - Performance - Acting, entertaining, disguises - Persuasion - Convincing, negotiating - Streetwise - Urban survival, criminal contacts - Subterfuge - Lying, deception, misdirection

    Mental Skills: - Academics - Humanities, history, general knowledge - Awareness - Noticing details, perception - Finance - Money management, economics - Investigation - Finding clues, forensics, research - Medicine - Treating wounds, diagnosis - Occult - Supernatural knowledge - Politics - Power structures, bureaucracy - Science - Natural sciences, chemistry, physics - Technology - Computers, electronics, hacking

    Essential Skills¶

    Everyone Should Have: - Awareness 2-3 - Notice threats and clues - Athletics 1-2 - Basic physical competence - One combat skill 2-3 - Survive fights

    Combat Characters Need: - Firearms 3-4 OR Melee 3-4 - Athletics 2-3 - Dodge and maneuver - Awareness 2-3 - Spot enemies

    Social Characters Need: - Persuasion 3-4 OR Intimidation 3-4 - Insight 2-3 - Read people - Subterfuge 2-3 - Maintain covers

    Investigation Characters Need: - Investigation 3-4 - Find clues - Awareness 3-4 - Notice details - Occult 2-3 - Understand threats

    Specialty Planning¶

    You get 3 specialties to start.

    How Specialties Work¶

    When you use a specialty: 1. Roll your dice pool normally 2. Count successes as usual 3. If you succeed, reroll any 1s once 4. Add any new successes from rerolled dice

    Example: - Roll Firearms 3 + Dexterity 3 = 6 dice - Result: [7][4][1][1][9][3] = 3 successes - Specialty: Firearms (Pistols) applies - Reroll the two 1s: [6][2] = 1 additional success - Final: 4 successes total

    Specialty Rules¶

    Requirements: - Skill must have 1+ dots - Can have multiple specialties per skill - Max specialties per skill = skill dots

    Best Practices: - Put specialties on your most-used skills - Narrow focus increases applicability - Coordinate with your concept

    Strategic Specialty Selection¶

    Combat Character: - Firearms (Pistols) or Firearms (Rifles) - Athletics (Dodging) - Stealth (Ambushes)

    Social Character: - Persuasion (Negotiations) - Subterfuge (Maintaining Cover) - Insight (Detecting Lies)

    Investigation Character: - Investigation (Crime Scenes) - Awareness (Noticing Details) - Occult (Vampires) or Occult (Specific threat)

    Specialty Examples by Skill¶

    Firearms: - Pistols, Rifles, Shotguns, Quick Draw, Moving Targets

    Athletics: - Running, Climbing, Dodging, Jumping, Swimming

    Investigation: - Crime Scenes, Research, Forensics, Tracking, Interviews

    Persuasion: - Negotiations, Fast Talk, Seduction, Inspiration

    Occult: - Vampires, Werewolves, Ghosts, Demons, Rituals

    Medicine: - Emergency Care, Surgery, Diagnosis, Toxicology

    Creed and Drive¶

    Your Creed and Drive define who you are as a Hunter.

    The Five Creeds¶

    Defender - Philosophy: Protect the innocent from supernatural harm - Fields: Investigation, protection, community defense - Best For: Guardians, protectors, police officers - Drives: Virtue, Ambition, Competition

    Innocent - Philosophy: Stumbled into the truth, learning as you go - Fields: Adaptation, survival, discovery - Best For: Reluctant heroes, ordinary people thrust into danger - Drives: Curiosity, Vice, Rebellion

    Redeemer - Philosophy: Save the corrupted, redeem the fallen - Fields: Compassion, understanding, mercy - Best For: Priests, counselors, idealists - Drives: Virtue, Ambition, Desire

    Avenger - Philosophy: Punish the guilty, destroy the wicked - Fields: Justice, retribution, judgment - Best For: Soldiers, vigilantes, survivors - Drives: Vengeance, Competition, Rebellion

    Zealot - Philosophy: Eradicate all supernatural darkness - Fields: Extremism, purification, total war - Best For: Fanatics, extremists, true believers - Drives: Ambition, Vengeance, Virtue

    The Seven Drives¶

    Ambition - Achieve greatness through the Hunt Competition - Prove yourself the best Hunter Curiosity - Uncover the truth about the supernatural Desire - Fulfill a specific want or need Rebellion - Reject authority and societal norms Vengeance - Hurt those who hurt you or others Vice - Indulge in pleasures or addictions Virtue - Uphold moral principles and ethics

    Creed + Drive Combinations¶

    Defender + Virtue: "I protect because it's right." - Classic heroic guardian - Strong moral compass - Self-sacrificing

    Avenger + Vengeance: "They killed my family. I'll kill them all." - Dark vigilante - Driven by loss - Brutal efficiency

    Innocent + Curiosity: "I need to understand what's happening." - Reluctant investigator - Learning the truth - Growth-focused

    Redeemer + Desire: "I want to save my sister from the vampires." - Personal mission - Compassionate approach - Specific goal

    Zealot + Ambition: "I'll lead the crusade against darkness." - Fanatical leader - Grand vision - Uncompromising

    Setting Creed and Drive¶

    /creed creed:Defender
/drive drive:Virtue
/ambition ambition:Protect my neighborhood from all supernatural threats
/desire desire:Find and stop the vampire preying on locals

    Ambition = Long-term goal Desire = Immediate short-term focus

    Edge Selection¶

    Every Hunter starts with one Edge.

    Edge Categories¶

    Assets (Resources and Connections): - Arsenal - Weapons and military equipment - Contacts - Network of allies and informants - Fame - Celebrity status and recognition - Library - Knowledge and research resources - Safehouse - Secure location and haven - Status - Authority and organizational power

    Aptitudes (Active Abilities): - Fleet - Enhanced speed and agility - Inspire - Bolster and lead allies - Martyr - Sacrifice for others - Pickpocket - Steal and misdirect - Scrounger - Find resources anywhere - Senses - Enhanced perception

    Endowments (Supernatural Powers): - Artifact - Mystical object with power - Benediction - Divine protection and blessing - Global Access - Information network - Sense Danger - Precognition and warnings - Third Eye - See the supernatural

    Starter Edge Recommendations¶

    Combat Character: - Arsenal - Always armed and equipped - Fleet - Superior mobility in fights - Senses - Never surprised

    Social Character: - Contacts - Information and favors - Fame - Leverage celebrity - Status - Authority and power

    Investigation Character: - Library - Research anything - Senses - Notice everything - Third Eye - See the truth

    Support Character: - Inspire - Buff your team - Martyr - Protect others - Benediction - Heal and bless

    Edge Synergies¶

    Arsenal + Avenger: - Heavily armed vengeance - Military precision - Direct combat

    Contacts + Defender: - Community protection network - Information gathering - Social support

    Library + Innocent: - Research to understand - Knowledge is power - Learning the truth

    Senses + Defender: - Detect threats early - Protect proactively - Enhanced awareness

    Optimization Tips¶

    Common Mistakes¶

    Don't spread too thin: - Many skills at 1 dot - Few skills at 3-4 dots

    Don't neglect awareness: - Awareness 0 - Awareness 2-3 minimum

    Don't forget combat: - No combat skills - At least Firearms 2 or Brawl 2

    Don't ignore Willpower: - Composure 1, Resolve 1 (Willpower 2) - At least one at 2-3 (Willpower 4-5)

    Optimization Strategies¶

    Maximize primary pools: - Identify your character's main role - Get primary attribute + skill = 6-8 dice - Example: Dexterity 4 + Firearms 4 = 8 dice

    Ensure minimums: - Health 5+ (Stamina 2+) - Willpower 4+ (Composure + Resolve = 4+) - Awareness 2+ - One combat skill 2+

    Plan progression: - Know what you'll raise first - Attributes are expensive (new 4 XP) - Skills are cheaper (new 2 XP) - Specialties are cheap (3 XP each)

    Coordinate with team: - Don't duplicate roles - Cover each other's weaknesses - Share information and resources

    Power Gaming Considerations¶

    Maximum combat effectiveness: - Dexterity 4, Stamina 3 minimum - Firearms 4 or Melee 4 - Athletics 3 - Arsenal or Fleet Edge

    Maximum social influence: - Charisma or Manipulation 4 - Persuasion 4 or Subterfuge 4 - Insight 3 - Contacts, Fame, or Status Edge

    Maximum investigation: - Intelligence 4, Wits 3 - Investigation 4, Awareness 3 - Occult 2-3 - Library or Senses Edge
