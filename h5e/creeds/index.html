    Creeds & Drives¶

    Your Creed defines your hunting philosophy and methods. Your Drive explains why you hunt. Together, they shape your character's identity and provide mechanical benefits through the Redemption system.

    What are Creeds?¶

    Creeds are the five philosophies that guide Hunters. Each Creed represents a different approach to hunting the supernatural.

    The Five Creeds¶

    • Entrepreneurial üî® - Building, inventing, and creating solutions
    • Faithful ‚úùÔ∏è - Direct confrontation backed by belief
    • Inquisitive üîç - Gaining knowledge and information
    • Martial ‚öîÔ∏è - Physical conflict and combat
    • Underground üé≠ - Stealth, subterfuge, and deception

    Creed Descriptions¶

    Entrepreneurial üî®¶

    Building solutions to supernatural problems

    You believe in preparation, resources, and creating the right tools for the job. Whether it's building weapons, establishing safe houses, or creating infrastructure for the hunt, you solve problems through construction and innovation.

    Philosophy: - "Give me the right tools and I can destroy any monster" - Preparation beats improvisation - Build systems, not quick fixes - Resources win wars

    Field of Expertise: - Building and inventing - Repairing and maintaining - Creating infrastructure - Resource management - Establishing networks

    Typical Actions: - Crafting specialized weapons - Setting up safe houses - Building communication networks - Creating supply chains - Designing traps and defenses

    Herald Command: 

    /creed action:set
(Select Entrepreneurial)

    Faithful ‚úùÔ∏è¶

    Direct conflict guided by belief

    You hunt because it's right. Faith - in a deity, a cause, or humanity itself - gives you strength to confront evil directly. You don't hide or scheme; you stand in the light and fight.

    Philosophy: - "Evil must be confronted, not avoided" - Faith provides strength - Direct action over complex plans - Stand and fight

    Field of Expertise: - Direct confrontation - Open conflict - Rallying others - Moral leadership - Fearless action

    Typical Actions: - Kicking in doors - Public denunciations - Protecting the innocent directly - Leading charges - Inspiring others through example

    Herald Command: 

    /creed action:set
(Select Faithful)

    Inquisitive üîç¶

    Knowledge is power

    You hunt through information. Understanding your enemy - their weaknesses, patterns, and nature - is more valuable than any weapon. You research, investigate, and study before you strike.

    Philosophy: - "Know your enemy better than yourself" - Information wins battles - Research before action - Every monster has a weakness

    Field of Expertise: - Research and investigation - Pattern analysis - Gathering intelligence - Occult knowledge - Strategic planning

    Typical Actions: - Extensive research before hunts - Building databases of monster lore - Interrogating witnesses - Analyzing evidence - Finding weaknesses

    Herald Command: 

    /creed action:set
(Select Inquisitive)

    Martial ‚öîÔ∏è¶

    Physical conflict is inevitable

    You are a warrior. Monsters are enemies to be destroyed through superior tactics, training, and firepower. You study combat, train constantly, and engage the enemy with lethal force.

    Philosophy: - "Train hard, fight easy" - Combat solves problems - Tactical superiority wins - Overwhelming force ends threats

    Field of Expertise: - Physical combat - Weapons expertise - Tactical planning - Hand-to-hand fighting - Military operations

    Typical Actions: - Combat training - Weapon drills - Tactical planning - Ambushes and assaults - Coordinated strikes

    Herald Command: 

    /creed action:set
(Select Martial)

    Underground üé≠¶

    Shadows and secrets

    You hunt from the shadows. Stealth, deception, and subterfuge are your weapons. You infiltrate, gather intelligence covertly, and strike when your enemy least expects it.

    Philosophy: - "They can't fight what they can't see" - Stealth beats strength - Information through infiltration - Strike from the shadows

    Field of Expertise: - Stealth and infiltration - Deception and disguise - Covert operations - Social engineering - Hidden movements

    Typical Actions: - Infiltrating monster organizations - Gathering intelligence covertly - Assassination and sabotage - Using disguises - Building spy networks

    Herald Command: 

    /creed action:set
(Select Underground)

    What are Drives?¶

    Drives are the emotional or psychological reasons you hunt. While your Creed is how you hunt, your Drive is why you hunt.

    Each Drive has an associated Redemption - the action that can restore you when you enter Despair.

    The Seven Drives¶

    • Curiosity üîç - You hunt to learn
    • Vengeance ‚öîÔ∏è - You hunt to punish
    • Oath ü§ù - You hunt because you swore to
    • Greed üí∞ - You hunt for profit
    • Pride üëë - You hunt to prove yourself
    • Envy üíö - You hunt what you can't have
    • Atonement üïäÔ∏è - You hunt to redeem yourself

    Drive & Redemption Details¶

    Curiosity üîç¶

    Drive: You hunt to understand the supernatural

    You need to know. What are vampires? How do werewolves work? What makes ghosts persist? The mystery of the supernatural compels you to hunt and study it.

    Redemption: Uncover new information about your quarry

    When in Despair, learning something genuinely new about the monsters you hunt can restore your Drive.

    Example: - Discovering a vampire's true name - Learning how a specific werewolf pack operates - Finding an ancient text about your prey - Understanding a monster's weakness

    Herald Commands: 

    /drive action:set
(Select Curiosity)

# After learning new information while in Despair:
/redemption

    Vengeance ‚öîÔ∏è¶

    Drive: You hunt to hurt those who hurt you

    They took something from you - family, friends, your normal life. Now you hunt to make them pay. Every monster you hurt is justice for what was taken.

    Redemption: Hurt your quarry

    When in Despair, successfully injuring or damaging your prey can restore your Drive.

    Example: - Wounding the vampire that killed your family - Destroying a monster's haven - Killing one of their allies - Inflicting meaningful harm

    Herald Commands: 

    /drive action:set
(Select Vengeance)

# After hurting your quarry while in Despair:
/redemption

    Oath ü§ù¶

    Drive: You swore to hunt, and you keep your word

    You made a promise - to a dying friend, to yourself, to a higher power. Your oath binds you to the hunt, and you will not break faith.

    Redemption: Actively uphold or fulfill your oath

    When in Despair, recommitting to your oath through meaningful action can restore your Drive.

    Example: - Honoring a promise you made - Protecting what you swore to protect - Following through on a commitment - Staying true to your word under pressure

    Herald Commands: 

    /drive action:set
(Select Oath)

# After upholding your oath while in Despair:
/redemption

    Greed üí∞¶

    Drive: You hunt for wealth, power, or resources

    Monsters have resources - money, artifacts, territory. You hunt to take what they have. Maybe you need it, maybe you want it, but you're going to get it.

    Redemption: Acquire resources from your enemies

    When in Despair, successfully taking something valuable from monsters can restore your Drive.

    Example: - Looting a vampire's treasure - Claiming a monster's territory - Stealing a supernatural artifact - Taking control of their assets

    Herald Commands: 

    /drive action:set
(Select Greed)

# After acquiring resources while in Despair:
/redemption

    Pride üëë¶

    Drive: You hunt to prove you're the best

    You need to be the best Hunter, to excel, to prove your superiority. Every successful hunt proves you're better than the monsters - and better than other Hunters.

    Redemption: Best your quarry in a contest

    When in Despair, defeating your prey in a meaningful contest of skill can restore your Drive.

    Example: - Outsmarting a cunning vampire - Defeating a monster in single combat - Winning a battle of wits - Proving superior in your specialty

    Herald Commands: 

    /drive action:set
(Select Pride)

# After besting your quarry while in Despair:
/redemption

    Envy üíö¶

    Drive: You hunt what you can't have

    Monsters have what you want - immortality, power, freedom from human weakness. You hate them for having what you lack, and hunting them is an expression of that envy.

    Redemption: Ally with your quarry

    When in Despair, finding common ground or forming a temporary alliance with monsters can restore your Drive.

    Example: - Working with a vampire against a greater threat - Understanding a monster's perspective - Finding shared interests - Temporary cooperation

    Herald Commands: 

    /drive action:set
(Select Envy)

# After allying with your quarry while in Despair:
/redemption

    Atonement üïäÔ∏è¶

    Drive: You hunt to redeem past failures

    You failed someone, or you were a monster yourself once. You hunt to make up for what you've done, to protect others from suffering as you or your victims did.

    Redemption: Protect someone from your quarry

    When in Despair, successfully saving someone from monsters can restore your Drive.

    Example: - Saving a victim from a vampire - Protecting innocents from supernatural threats - Preventing someone from suffering your fate - Shielding others from harm

    Herald Commands: 

    /drive action:set
(Select Atonement)

# After protecting someone while in Despair:
/redemption

    Creed & Drive Combinations¶

    Your Creed and Drive work together to define your character:

    Example Combinations¶

    Martial + Vengeance: A combat-focused warrior hunting the monsters that killed their family. Direct, aggressive, and uncompromising.

    Inquisitive + Curiosity: A researcher obsessed with understanding the supernatural. Knowledge for knowledge's sake, backed by thorough investigation.

    Underground + Greed: A covert operative who infiltrates monster organizations to steal their resources. Practical and profit-driven.

    Faithful + Atonement: A former sinner seeking redemption through protecting others. Driven by guilt and guided by moral conviction.

    Entrepreneurial + Oath: A builder who swore to create safe havens for humanity. Methodical, prepared, and honor-bound.

    Managing Creeds & Drives in Herald¶

    Setting Your Creed¶

    /creed action:set

    Herald displays interactive buttons for all five Creeds. Click to select.

    Viewing Your Creed¶

    /creed action:view

    Shows your current Creed and its field of expertise.

    Setting Your Drive¶

    /drive action:set

    Herald displays interactive buttons for all seven Drives with their Redemptions. Select one.

    Viewing Your Drive¶

    /drive action:view

    Shows your current Drive and Redemption path.

    Ambition & Desire¶

    In addition to your Drive, you have:

    Ambition (Long-term goal)¶

    Your Ambition is a major, long-term objective that drives your character forward.

    Mechanical Benefit: - Making progress toward your Ambition recovers 1 Aggravated Willpower

    Examples: - "Destroy the vampire court that rules this city" - "Build a network of Hunter safe houses nationwide" - "Discover the truth about what created Hunters" - "Protect my family from all supernatural threats"

    Herald Command: 

    /ambition ambition:"Destroy the vampire elder who killed my mentor"

    Desire (Short-term goal)¶

    Your Desire is a immediate, achievable objective.

    Mechanical Benefit: - Accomplishing your Desire recovers 1 Superficial Willpower

    Examples: - "Find out where the vampires are hiding" - "Acquire better weapons for the cell" - "Learn the werewolf's true name" - "Protect the witness until the trial"

    Herald Command: 

    /desire desire:"Identify the creature killing homeless people"

    Tip: Update your Desire frequently as you accomplish goals. It should change session-to-session.

    Using Drives for Recovery¶

    Normal Recovery (Not in Despair)¶

    Pursuing your Drive provides narrative direction and can grant bonuses (Storyteller discretion).

    Recovery from Despair¶

    When you're in Despair, completing your Redemption is the ONLY way to recover:

    1. Enter Despair (through failed Desperation roll or choice)
    2. Work toward your Redemption
    3. Complete it (Storyteller confirms)
    4. Use /redemption command
    5. Drive restored, Despair removed

    Example: 

    State: In Despair
Drive: Vengeance
Redemption: Hurt your quarry

Action: Successfully ambush and wound the vampire elder

Storyteller: "You definitely hurt him. That's Redemption."

Command: /redemption

Result: Despair removed, can use Desperation again
