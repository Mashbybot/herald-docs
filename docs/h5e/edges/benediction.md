Divine power flows through you. Blessings heal, faith protects, prayer destroys evil.

Benediction represents a Hunter's connection to divine forces that grant supernatural protection and healing. Whether through prayer, faith, or divine favor, you channel celestial power to shield allies, heal wounds, and smite the wicked.

---

## Overview

### What is Benediction?

Benediction is an **Endowment Edge** that grants divine supernatural abilities focused on protection, healing, and blessing. You are touched by higher powers - your faith is rewarded with genuine miracles that manifest as protective auras, healing light, and divine judgment.

The Edge operates through **divine channeling**: you are a conduit for celestial power. Through prayer, faith, or ritual, you invoke blessings that protect the righteous and harm the profane.

### Who Should Take This Edge?

**Ideal For:**
- **Faithful Hunters** - Priests, clerics, believers
- **Healers** - Medical/support role
- **Defenders** - Protect through divine power
- **Redeemers** - Save souls with blessings
- **Holy warriors** - Faith and combat combined

**Consider Carefully If:**
- Your character lacks faith/spirituality
- Your chronicle doesn't support divine powers
- You prefer offensive/combat focus (Benediction is support-heavy)
- Storyteller doesn't want overt divine intervention

### Key Strengths

- **Healing** - Restore Health supernaturally
- **Protection** - Divine shields and blessings
- **Support** - Buff allies with celestial power
- **Purification** - Cleanse corruption and curses
- **Morale** - Inspire through visible miracles

### Limitations

- **Faith required** - Narratively tied to belief
- **Defensive focus** - Limited offensive capabilities
- **Willpower intensive** - Most powers cost Willpower
- **Divine judgment** - Powers may fail if used unworthily
- **Obvious supernatural** - Hard to hide divine miracles

## All Perks

### Healing Touch

**What It Does:**
Channel divine power to heal wounds. Touch an injured person and spend Willpower to restore Health equal to your Desperation rating.

**Mechanical Effect:**
- Action: Touch injured person
- Cost: 1 Willpower
- Healing: Restore Health = Desperation rating
- Can heal lethal and bashing damage
- Cannot heal aggravated (supernatural wounds)

**When To Use:**
- **Post-combat** - Heal wounded allies
- **Crisis healing** - Stabilize dying allies
- **Investigation** - Heal witnesses/victims to gain cooperation
- **Resource conservation** - Supernatural healing > mundane medicine

**Strategy Notes:**
- Foundation perk for Benediction
- Willpower-intensive (requires management)
- More effective at high Desperation
- Can't heal yourself (most variants)

**Example:**
```
Ally wounded in vampire fight: 4 Health remaining (of 7)
Healing Touch: Place hand on wound, pray
Spend 1 Willpower
Healing: Restore Desperation 3 = 3 Health
Ally restored to 7 Health (fully healed)
Divine light closes wounds
Grace through faith
```

**Herald Commands:**
```
/edge edge:Benediction
/perks edge:Benediction perk:Healing Touch
```

### Divine Shield

**What It Does:**
Invoke protective blessing on yourself or ally. Target gains temporary armor equal to your Desperation rating for one scene.

**Mechanical Effect:**
- Action: Invoke blessing (prayer/ritual)
- Cost: 1 Willpower
- Duration: One scene
- Effect: Target gains armor = Desperation (stacks with physical armor)
- Protects from all damage types

**When To Use:**
- **Pre-combat** - Bless allies before engagement
- **Tank buffing** - Enhance frontline fighters
- **Crisis protection** - Shield vulnerable ally under attack
- **Dangerous operations** - Prepare for known threats

**Strategy Notes:**
- Proactive defense (use before combat)
- Synergizes with high-Stamina allies
- Willpower cost = strategic resource allocation
- Scene duration = efficient use

**Example:**
```
Preparing to assault vampire haven
Divine Shield: Bless tank character
Cost: 1 Willpower
Effect: Target gains armor 3 (your Desperation)
Tank now has: Physical armor 2 + Divine Shield 3 = 5 total armor
Protected through faith for entire combat
Blessing visible as faint golden aura
```

**Herald Commands:**
```
/perks edge:Benediction perk:Divine Shield
```

### Purify

**What It Does:**
Cleanse supernatural corruption, curses, possession, or disease through divine power. Remove one supernatural affliction from target.

**What It Cleanses:**
- **Curses** - Remove hexes and supernatural maledictions
- **Possession** - Exorcise spirits from host
- **Disease** - Cure supernatural illness
- **Corruption** - Cleanse taint (vampire blood addiction, etc.)
- **Mental influence** - Break mesmerism, mind control

**When To Use:**
- **Curse removal** - Ally hexed by witch
- **Exorcism** - Possessed victim
- **Addiction breaking** - Vampire blood thrall
- **Mind control** - Break supernatural domination
- **Corruption cleansing** - Remove taint

**Strategy Notes:**
- Costs Willpower (1-3 depending on affliction severity)
- May require contested roll vs curse/possessor
- Narrative heavy (role-play the purification)
- Essential for non-combat supernatural threats

**Example:**
```
Ally possessed by malevolent spirit
Purify: Perform exorcism ritual
Cost: 2 Willpower (powerful spirit)
Contested roll: Manipulation 3 + Occult 4 + Desperation 3 = 10 dice
/roll pool:10 difficulty:4 comment:Exorcism vs spirit
Result: 5 successes, margin 1
Spirit expelled, ally freed
Divine light burns spirit from host
Salvation through faith
```

**Herald Commands:**
```
/perks edge:Benediction perk:Purify
```

### Ward

**What It Does:**
Create blessed barrier that supernatural entities cannot cross. Ward doorways, windows, or areas against intrusion.

**Ward Capabilities:**
- **Barrier:** Supernatural beings cannot cross
- **Detection:** Ward alerts you if tested/broken
- **Duration:** Until sunrise, or one week if permanent (higher cost)
- **Size:** Doorway, window, or small area (room)
- **Selective:** Can allow specific beings through

**When To Use:**
- **Safehouse protection** - Ward entrances against vampires
- **Investigation sites** - Protect crime scenes from spirits
- **Refuge creation** - Emergency sanctuaries
- **Trap preparation** - Force enemies through specific routes

**Strategy Notes:**
- Costs Willpower (1 for temporary, 3 for permanent)
- Preventive security measure
- Can be broken by sufficiently powerful entities (contested)
- Visible to supernatural sight (glowing threshold)

**Example:**
```
Vampires hunting your cell, need safe refuge
Ward: Bless safehouse entrances
Cost: 3 Willpower (permanent ward on 3 doors)
Effect: Vampires cannot enter without invitation
Attempted entry: Ward blazes, repels vampire
You're alerted (ward tested)
Sanctuary through divine protection
```

**Herald Commands:**
```
/perks edge:Benediction perk:Ward
```

### Smite Evil

**What It Does:**
Channel divine wrath into devastating attack against supernatural evil. Your attack deals additional aggravated damage equal to your Desperation rating against supernatural creatures.

**Mechanical Effect:**
- Action: Invoke blessing on weapon/attack
- Cost: 1 Willpower
- Duration: One attack
- Bonus damage: Add Desperation as aggravated damage (in addition to rolled damage)
- Only affects supernatural/evil targets

**When To Use:**
- **Boss fights** - Devastating damage spike
- **Demon/vampire combat** - Smite the unholy
- **Critical strikes** - Ensure kill
- **Divine judgment** - Destroy evil utterly

**Strategy Notes:**
- Offensive Benediction perk (most are defensive)
- Willpower-gated burst damage
- Best with high Desperation
- Narrative weight (you are instrument of judgment)

**Example:**
```
Final strike against vampire lord
Smite Evil: Invoke divine wrath
Cost: 1 Willpower
Attack: Melee 5 + Str 4 = 9 dice
/roll pool:9 difficulty:2 comment:Divine smite strike
Result: 5 successes = 3 damage
Smite Evil: +3 aggravated (Desperation 3)
Total: 6 aggravated damage
Vampire lord destroyed in holy fire
Judgment delivered through faith
```

**Herald Commands:**
```
/perks edge:Benediction perk:Smite Evil
```

## Strategy Guide

### Early Game (Characters 0-20 XP)

**Starting with Benediction:**
- **Focus:** Basic healing and protection
- **Best Perks:** Healing Touch, Divine Shield
- **Build:** Composure/Resolve 3+ (Willpower pool), Occult 3
- **Role:** Healer and support

**Recommended Build:**
- Composure 3, Resolve 3 (Willpower 6)
- Manipulation 3 (invoke blessings)
- Medicine 3, Occult 3, Awareness 2
- Healing Touch first (post-combat healing)

**Support Pattern:**
- Pre-combat: Divine Shield on tank
- During combat: Minimal action (let others fight)
- Post-combat: Healing Touch on wounded
- Investigation: Purify supernatural afflictions

### Mid Game (Characters 21-50 XP)

**Developing Benediction:**
- **Add Perks:** Purify, Ward
- **Expand Role:** Healer + protector + purifier
- **Willpower Management:** Raise Composure/Resolve
- **Team Integration:** Divine support specialist

**Advanced Tactics:**
- Ward safehouse and key locations
- Divine Shield proactively before danger
- Purify corruption and curses
- Healing Touch for major wounds
- Manage Willpower carefully (6-8 pool ideal)

**Progression:**
- Raise Composure and Resolve to 4 (Willpower 8)
- Raise Occult to 4-5 (understand divine mechanics)
- Raise Medicine to 4 (complement supernatural healing)
- Second Edge: Inspire (divine leader), Library (religious texts)

### Late Game (Characters 51+ XP)

**Mastering Benediction:**
- **All Perks:** Complete divine power
- **Smite Evil:** Offensive capability
- **High Willpower:** 8-10 pool for sustained miracles
- **Living Saint:** Known for miraculous works

**End-game Tactics:**
- Ward strategic locations permanently
- Divine Shield multiple allies
- Purify any corruption
- Healing Touch mid-combat (action economy improved)
- Smite Evil for boss kills
- Miracles on demand

**Ultimate Build:**
- Composure 5, Resolve 4 (Willpower 9)
- Manipulation 4, Occult 5
- All Benediction perks
- Second/Third Edge: Inspire, Artifact (holy relic), Status (religious authority)
- Miracle worker and holy warrior

### Edge Synergies

**Works Great With:**
- **Redeemer Creed** - Save and heal theme
- **Defender Creed** - Protect through divine power
- **Zealot Creed** - Holy crusader
- **Inspire Edge** - Divine leader healing and empowering
- **Artifact Edge** - Holy relic + divine powers
- **Status Edge** - Religious authority + supernatural power
- **High Willpower** - Sustain miracles

**Conflicts With:**
- **Faithless concepts** - Benediction requires belief
- **Selfish builds** - Benediction is support-focused
- **Avenger (sometimes)** - Vengeance vs mercy

**Neutral:**
- **Arsenal, Contacts, Fleet** - Different focus but compatible

### Using Benediction in Play

Benediction operates through faith and Willpower:

1. **Invoke power** - Declare which Benediction perk
2. **Spend Willpower** - Pay cost (1-3 Willpower depending on perk)
3. **Roll if needed** - Some perks auto-succeed, others require roll
4. **Describe miracle** - Narrate divine manifestation
5. **Apply effect** - Healing, protection, purification, etc.

**Example:**
```
Ally critically wounded: 2 Health remaining, dying
You: "I invoke Healing Touch - placing hands on their wounds and praying"
Cost: 1 Willpower (you have 7, now 6)
Healing: Desperation 4 = restore 4 Health
Ally: 2 â†’ 6 Health, stabilized
Describe: "Divine light flows from your hands, wounds knitting shut impossibly fast"
Miracle witnessed by all
```

**Willpower Management:**
```
Your Willpower: 7/7 (Composure 4 + Resolve 3)
Pre-combat:
- Divine Shield on Tank: -1 Willpower (6/7 remaining)
- Divine Shield on Striker: -1 Willpower (5/7 remaining)

Combat: Minimal Willpower use (save for emergencies)

Post-combat:
- Healing Touch on Ally 1: -1 Willpower (4/7)
- Healing Touch on Ally 2: -1 Willpower (3/7)
- Purify curse on Ally 3: -2 Willpower (1/7)

End scene: Willpower regenerates to full (7/7) for next scene
Strategic resource management critical
```
