Arsenal represents a Hunter's access to weapons, military equipment, and armaments. Whether through military connections, black market contacts, or supernatural providence, you always have the tools of war at your disposal.

## Overview

### What is Arsenal?

Arsenal is an **Asset Edge** that grants access to weapons, ammunition, armor, and military equipment. You can procure firearms, explosives, tactical gear, and specialized armaments that would be difficult or impossible for civilians to obtain.

The Edge operates on a simple principle: **you always have what you need when you need it**. Run out of ammo mid-fight? Arsenal provides. Need a specific weapon for a vampire lord? Arsenal delivers. Want untraceable gear? Arsenal ensures it.

### Who Should Take This Edge?

**Ideal For:**
- **Combat specialists** - Soldiers, mercenaries, fighters
- **Avengers** - Armed vengeance seekers
- **Zealots** - Holy warriors with righteous fury
- **Tactical Hunters** - Military-minded strategists

**Consider Carefully If:**
- Your chronicle focuses on social intrigue (weapons won't help)
- Storyteller restricts weapon access heavily
- You prefer supernatural/mystical Edges
- Combat is rare in your game

### Key Strengths

- **Always armed** - Never caught without a weapon
- **Equipment advantage** - Superior firepower over enemies
- **Team support** - Equip your entire cell
- **Tactical flexibility** - Right tool for every threat
- **Professional grade** - Military-quality equipment

### Limitations

- **Doesn't improve skill** - Still need Firearms/Melee dots
- **Requires justification** - Must explain procurement
- **Attention risk** - Heavy weapons draw notice
- **Situation-dependent** - Useless in purely social scenes
- **Storyteller discretion** - What you can access varies

## All Perks

### Team Requisition

**What It Does:**
When you successfully procure a weapon using Arsenal, you can provide copies of that weapon to your allies equal to your margin of success.

**Mechanical Effect:**
- Roll to acquire weapon (usually Manipulation + Streetwise or relevant skill)
- Margin = Successes - Difficulty
- Provide that many copies to allies

**When To Use:**
- **Before major combat** - Arm your entire cell
- **Assault missions** - Everyone needs the same weapon
- **Tactical operations** - Coordinated loadouts
- **Emergency situations** - Quick mass armament

**Strategy Notes:**
- Best with high Manipulation or Streetwise
- Coordinate with your cell on needed weapons
- Plan equipment before missions
- Build margin by specializing in procurement

**Example:**
```
You need shotguns for a ghoul nest assault.
Roll: Manipulation 3 + Streetwise 3 = 6 dice, Difficulty 2
Result: 4 successes, Margin 2
You acquire 3 shotguns (one for you + 2 for allies)
```

**Herald Commands:**
```
/edge edge:Arsenal
/perks edge:Arsenal perk:Team Requisition
```

### Special Features

**What It Does:**
Your weapons have special features, modifications, or enhancements. The number of features equals your margin of success when procuring the weapon.

**Possible Features:**
- **Silencer** - Suppressed fire, stealthy kills
- **Extended Magazine** - More rounds before reload
- **Scope** - Bonus to long-range attacks
- **Laser Sight** - Bonus to accuracy
- **Armor-Piercing Rounds** - Penetrate protection
- **Incendiary Rounds** - Fire damage
- **Silver Rounds** - Effective against werewolves
- **Wooden Stakes** - Effective against vampires
- **Blessed Ammunition** - Holy damage
- **Tactical Light** - Illuminate targets
- **Under-barrel Grenade Launcher** - Explosive option
- **Custom Grip** - Better handling

**When To Use:**
- **Specialized threats** - Tailor weapons to enemies
- **Tactical advantage** - Features for specific scenarios
- **Optimization** - Maximize weapon effectiveness
- **Style** - Cool factor and character flavor

**Strategy Notes:**
- Plan features based on known threats
- Silver for werewolves, stakes for vampires
- Silencers for stealth operations
- Scopes for sniper builds

**Example:**
```
Procuring sniper rifle for vampire assassination
Roll: 5 successes, Difficulty 2, Margin 3
Features: Scope (+1 accuracy), Silencer (stealth), Silver rounds (extra damage to werewolves if needed)
```

**Herald Commands:**
```
/perks edge:Arsenal perk:Special Features
```

### Exotics

**What It Does:**
You can procure rare, one-of-a-kind, or extremely difficult-to-obtain weapons. Military prototypes, historical artifacts, experimental technology, or black market rarities.

**Examples:**
- **Prototype Railgun** - Experimental energy weapon
- **Flamethrower** - Area denial and fire damage
- **Rocket Launcher** - Anti-vehicle and anti-structure
- **Minigun** - Suppressive fire, massive ammunition
- **Crossbow** - Silent, reusable, medieval
- **Katana** - Masterwork blade
- **Holy Relic Sword** - Blessed melee weapon
- **Vampiric Blade** - Drains life force
- **Ghostly Ammunition** - Affects incorporeal entities
- **EMP Device** - Disable electronics

**When To Use:**
- **Unique threats** - One-time specialized weapons
- **Climactic battles** - Bring out the big guns
- **Impossible odds** - Need extraordinary firepower
- **Storyteller approval** - Discuss availability

**Strategy Notes:**
- Save for major confrontations
- Discuss with Storyteller beforehand
- Justify narratively (how did you get this?)
- Consequences likely (attention, heat, investigation)

**Example:**
```
Fighting an ancient vampire lord in his castle
Procure: Blessed flamethrower
Justification: Former military chaplain contact
Effect: Fire damage + holy damage, area effect
```

**Herald Commands:**
```
/perks edge:Arsenal perk:Exotics
```

### Untraceable

**What It Does:**
Your weapons never lead back to you. Serial numbers are filed off, ballistic evidence is inconclusive, and ownership trails vanish. Law enforcement cannot connect you to your armaments through forensic investigation.

**Mechanical Effect:**
- Weapons leave no legal trail
- Ballistics don't match
- Purchase history erased
- No registration
- Shell casings untraceable

**When To Use:**
- **After firefights** - No legal consequences
- **Police investigations** - Avoid arrest
- **Maintaining cover** - Stay off radar
- **Public incidents** - No blowback

**Strategy Notes:**
- Essential for Defenders (protect identity)
- Critical for public-facing Hunters
- Pairs with Contacts Edge (police informants)
- Allows reckless weapon use without consequences

**Example:**
```
Shootout with ghouls in public park
Police recover shell casings and bullets
Ballistics come back inconclusive - no match to any weapon
Your Arsenal weapons are untraceable - no investigation
```

**Herald Commands:**
```
/perks edge:Arsenal perk:Untraceable
```

---

### Backup Piece

**What It Does:**
Once per scene, produce a hidden weapon that wasn't detected by searches, security, or enemies. Pull a holdout pistol, reveal a hidden knife, or produce emergency armament from seemingly nowhere.

**Mechanical Effect:**
- Once per scene (resets after combat/scene ends)
- Weapon must be concealable (pistol, knife, stake)
- Bypasses searches and security
- Narrative surprise element

**When To Use:**
- **Captured** - Disarmed but not helpless
- **Security checkpoints** - Get past metal detectors
- **Social events** - Armed at galas and meetings
- **Desperate moments** - Last-resort weapon

**Strategy Notes:**
- Save for emergencies
- Don't reveal unless necessary
- Pairs with Pickpocket or Subterfuge
- Great narrative moment

**Example:**
```
Captured by vampire coterie, disarmed and bound
Scene: Interrogation in vampire haven
Action: Produce hidden stake from boot
Result: Surprise attack, escape opportunity
```

**Herald Commands:**
```
/perks edge:Arsenal perk:Backup Piece
```

## Strategy Guide

### Early Game (Characters 0-20 XP)

**Starting with Arsenal:**
- **Focus:** Reliable armament for combat
- **Best Perk:** Team Requisition (arm your cell)
- **Equipment:** Standard firearms (pistol, shotgun, rifle)
- **Tactics:** Ensure everyone has weapons before fights

**Recommended Build:**
- Dexterity 3-4, Firearms 3-4
- Streetwise 2-3 (procurement rolls)
- Team Requisition Perk first
- Coordinate with cell equipment needs

**Common Mistakes:**
- Over-relying on exotic weapons (keep it simple)
- Not arming allies (Team Requisition first)
- Forgetting to narrate weapon acquisition

### Mid Game (Characters 21-50 XP)

**Developing Arsenal:**
- **Add Perks:** Special Features, Untraceable
- **Equipment Upgrades:** Modified weapons with features
- **Tactical Planning:** Match weapons to known threats
- **Team Coordination:** Specialized loadouts per member

**Strategy:**
- Silver rounds for werewolf campaigns
- Blessed weapons for demon threats
- Silenced weapons for stealth operations
- Armor-piercing for heavily protected enemies

**Progression:**
- Raise Firearms to 4-5 (maximize combat effectiveness)
- Raise Streetwise to 3-4 (better procurement)
- Buy second combat Edge (Fleet for mobility, Senses for awareness)

### Late Game (Characters 51+ XP)

**Mastering Arsenal:**
- **All Perks:** Complete Arsenal mastery
- **Exotics Access:** Bring out the heavy artillery
- **Team Armorer:** You supply the entire cell perfectly
- **Tactical Dominance:** Right tool for every job

**Advanced Tactics:**
- Pre-position weapon caches
- Have backup weapons for backups
- Tailor loadouts to specific missions
- Use Backup Piece for dramatic reversals

**End-game Build:**
- Dexterity 5, Firearms 5 (10 dice pool)
- Multiple weapon specialties
- Second Edge: Fleet, Senses, or Inspire
- Team leader role (equipment coordinator)

### Edge Synergies

**Works Great With:**
- **Avenger Creed** - Armed vengeance, military precision
- **Zealot Creed** - Holy warrior with blessed weapons
- **Fleet Edge** - Mobile weapons platform, hit-and-run
- **Inspire Edge** - Lead from the front, armed and armored
- **Contacts Edge** - Access to weapon dealers, military supply

**Conflicts With:**
- **Innocent Creed** - Hard to justify military arsenal as civilian
- **Social-focused chronicles** - Weapons don't help persuasion
- **Stealth-heavy campaigns** - Loud guns draw attention

**Neutral:**
- **Library, Third Eye, Benediction** - Different focus but compatible

```
/perks edge:Arsenal perk:Exotics

/perks edge:Arsenal perk:Backup Piece
```

### Viewing Your Arsenal

```
/sheet
```

Your Arsenal Edge and Perks display in the Edges section of your character sheet.

### Using Arsenal in Play

Arsenal operates narratively. When you need a weapon:

1. **Describe what you need** - "I need silver bullets for werewolves"
2. **Storyteller sets difficulty** - Usually 2-3
3. **Roll procurement** - Manipulation + Streetwise typically
4. **Apply Perks** - Team Requisition for copies, Special Features for modifications
5. **Narrate acquisition** - Where/how you got it

**Example Sequence:**
```
You: "I need to arm the cell with assault rifles for the ghoul nest assault."
Storyteller: "Okay, Manipulation + Streetwise, Difficulty 3"
/roll pool:6 difficulty:3 comment:Procuring assault rifles
Result: 5 successes, Margin 2
With Team Requisition: 3 assault rifles (you + 2 allies)
With Special Features: Each has extended mags and tactical lights
```

## Quick Reference

| Perk | Effect Summary |
|------|----------------|
| **Team Requisition** | Provide weapon copies = margin of success |
| **Special Features** | Weapons have modifications = margin |
| **Exotics** | Procure rare/unique/experimental weapons |
| **Untraceable** | Weapons never lead back to you legally |
| **Backup Piece** | Once per scene, produce hidden weapon |

### Procurement Guidelines

**Standard Weapons (Difficulty 2):**
- Pistols, shotguns, hunting rifles
- Knives, bats, improvised weapons
- Standard ammunition

**Military Weapons (Difficulty 3):**
- Assault rifles, SMGs
- Combat shotguns
- Body armor
- Special ammunition (silver, blessed)

**Exotic Weapons (Difficulty 4-5):**
- Sniper rifles, machine guns
- Explosives, grenades
- Prototype technology
- Historical/magical weapons
