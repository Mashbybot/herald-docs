# Fame ‚≠ê

Everyone knows your name. Everyone knows your face. Use it.

Fame represents a Hunter's celebrity status and public recognition. Whether you're a local personality, social media influencer, professional athlete, actor, musician, or public figure, people recognize you - and that recognition is power.

---

## Overview

### What is Fame?

Fame is an **Asset Edge** that grants the benefits and burdens of public recognition. You're a known figure with media presence, public platform, and the ability to leverage your celebrity for influence, access, and resources.

The Edge operates on **social capital through recognition**: people know who you are, care what you think, and want to associate with you. This opens doors, grants audiences, and provides influence - but also means you're always being watched.

### Who Should Take This Edge?

**Ideal For:**
- **Social characters** - Charisma/Manipulation builds
- **Public-facing concepts** - Celebrities, influencers, athletes
- **Redeemers** - Use platform for good
- **Infiltrators** - Fame gets you anywhere
- **Media manipulators** - Control narratives

**Consider Carefully If:**
- Your concept is covert/secretive (Fame conflicts with stealth)
- You prefer staying under the radar
- Your Storyteller doesn't want celebrity mechanics
- Fame doesn't fit the chronicle tone

### Key Strengths

- **Instant recognition** - Everyone knows you
- **Access everywhere** - VIP treatment automatically
- **Social influence** - People listen to celebrities
- **Media platform** - Shape public opinion
- **Resources** - Money and connections through fame

### Limitations

- **Always watched** - No privacy, hard to operate covertly
- **Reputation matters** - Scandals damage effectiveness
- **Context-dependent** - Fame doesn't help in all situations
- **High maintenance** - Must maintain public image
- **Target** - Enemies know where to find you

## All Perks

### Instant Recognition

**What It Does:**
People immediately recognize you and treat you with deference, excitement, or favoritism. You receive VIP treatment, bypass minor obstacles through celebrity status, and get people's attention automatically.

**Mechanical Effect:**
- Automatic positive first impression
- Bypass queues, waiting, minor bureaucracy
- People want to help/please you
- Advantage on initial social rolls

**When To Use:**
- **Getting past security** - "Don't you know who I am?"
- **Social situations** - Instant credibility
- **Gaining audiences** - Meet important people
- **Resource access** - VIP service

**Strategy Notes:**
- Works best in your area of fame (local celebrity vs. international star)
- May backfire (sometimes you want anonymity)
- Context matters (rural area may not recognize you)
- Pairs with Charisma and Persuasion

**Example:**
```
Trying to get into exclusive club to meet a contact
Normally: Long wait, strict door policy
With Instant Recognition: Bouncer recognizes you, immediate entry, comp table
Door opens automatically because you're famous
```

**Herald Commands:**
```
/edge edge:Fame
/perks edge:Fame perk:Instant Recognition
```

### Media Platform

**What It Does:**
You have access to media channels - social media following, press contacts, broadcast opportunities - allowing you to shape narratives, spread information, or control public perception.

**Capabilities:**
- **Social Media** - Large following, viral potential
- **Press Access** - Journalists take your calls
- **Interviews** - Get on TV, radio, podcasts
- **Public Statements** - People listen when you speak
- **Trend Setting** - Influence public opinion

**When To Use:**
- **Control narratives** - Shape how events are perceived
- **Expose enemies** - Public accusations
- **Rally support** - Mobilize public action
- **Cover operations** - Misdirect attention
- **Warnings** - Inform public of threats (carefully)

**Strategy Notes:**
- Powerful but risky (draws attention)
- Can backfire (enemies use media against you)
- Coordinate with Storyteller on impact
- Use strategically, not constantly

**Example:**
```
Vampire coterie operating in city
You post social media thread about "suspicious disappearances"
Goes viral, public awareness increases
Forces vampires to be more careful (harder for them to hunt)
Or: Use platform to discredit rival Hunter cell
```

**Herald Commands:**
```
/perks edge:Fame perk:Media Platform
```

### Entourage

**What It Does:**
You have a group of loyal followers, fans, assistants, or hangers-on who can provide support, assistance, or cover. They're not combat-trained, but they're devoted to you.

**Entourage Members:**
- **Personal assistant** - Handles logistics
- **Manager** - Deals with business
- **Bodyguard** - Basic protection (not supernatural combat)
- **Fans** - Provide alibis, information, support
- **Technical crew** - Social media management

**When To Use:**
- **Logistics** - Someone else handles details
- **Cover stories** - Alibis and explanations
- **Information** - Fans are everywhere, see things
- **Resources** - Pool money for causes
- **Distractions** - Crowds provide cover

**Strategy Notes:**
- Entourage are civilians (don't send them into danger)
- They can be targeted by enemies
- Must maintain loyalty (treat them well)
- Useful for non-combat support

**Example:**
```
Need to disappear for a day (hunting vampires)
Entourage: Manager provides cover story (sick, vacation)
Fans create alibi (saw you at different location via social media)
Personal assistant handles cancellations and rescheduling
You're free to Hunt
```

**Herald Commands:**
```
/perks edge:Fame perk:Entourage
```

### Favor Trade

**What It Does:**
Use your celebrity status to call in favors from other celebrities, influencers, or powerful people who want association with you. Trade endorsements for assistance.

**Types of Favors:**
- **Celebrity Introductions** - Meet other famous people
- **Endorsement Trades** - "I'll promote you if you help me"
- **Resource Access** - Borrow money, equipment, venues
- **Information** - Other celebrities' secrets and connections
- **Support** - Public backing for causes

**When To Use:**
- **High-level access** - Meet the powerful
- **Resources** - Need money or equipment
- **Influence campaigns** - Get celebrity support
- **Information warfare** - Celebrity gossip networks

**Strategy Notes:**
- Limited use (don't burn relationships)
- Must offer value (what's in it for them?)
- Higher Fame = better favors available
- Coordinate with Storyteller on who you can access

**Example:**
```
Need to meet billionaire tech CEO (suspected vampire ally)
You're a famous athlete/actor
Favor Trade: "I'll appear at your charity gala if you give me 15 minutes"
CEO agrees (wants the publicity)
You get face-to-face meeting
```

**Herald Commands:**
```
/perks edge:Fame perk:Favor Trade
```

### Reputation Management

**What It Does:**
You can control and repair your public image, deflect scandals, and maintain your reputation even when things go wrong. Your PR team is exceptional.

**Capabilities:**
- **Damage Control** - Minimize scandal impact
- **Spin Stories** - Reframe narratives
- **Kill Stories** - Suppress negative press
- **Image Crafting** - Maintain desired public persona
- **Comeback** - Recover from public mistakes

**When To Use:**
- **After public incidents** - Cover up Hunt-related events
- **Scandal defense** - Accusations or exposure
- **Image maintenance** - Stay relevant and positive
- **Strategic rebranding** - Change public perception

**Strategy Notes:**
- Prevents Fame from becoming liability
- Essential if you have Despair/Desperation issues
- Requires resources (money, connections)
- Some things can't be spun (murder on camera, etc.)

**Example:**
```
You're caught on camera near vampire attack scene, covered in blood
Media frenzy brewing
Reputation Management activates:
PR team: "Charity volunteer helping victims, hero story"
Spin: You were giving first aid
Photos distributed: You in charity work (establish pattern)
Scandal defused, reputation intact
```

**Herald Commands:**
```
/perks edge:Fame perk:Reputation Management
```

## Strategy Guide

### Early Game (Characters 0-20 XP)

**Starting with Fame:**
- **Focus:** Leverage recognition for access
- **Best Perks:** Instant Recognition, Media Platform
- **Fame Level:** Local celebrity or rising star
- **Tactics:** Use fame to get through doors

**Recommended Build:**
- Charisma 4, Manipulation 3
- Persuasion 4, Performance 3, Subterfuge 2
- Instant Recognition first (immediate utility)
- Media Platform second (influence)

**Challenges:**
- Balancing fame and secret Hunting
- Maintaining cover story
- Not drawing attention to supernatural

### Mid Game (Characters 21-50 XP)

**Developing Fame:**
- **Add Perks:** Entourage, Favor Trade
- **Fame Level:** Regional/national recognition
- **Expand Platform:** Bigger media reach
- **Strategic Use:** Fame as weapon and shield

**Managing Fame:**
- Create separation between public persona and Hunt
- Use entourage for logistics and cover
- Build network through celebrity connections
- Control narratives proactively

**Progression:**
- Raise social skills (Persuasion, Performance to 5)
- Buy second Edge (Contacts for synergy, Status for institutional power)
- Invest in Charisma (4-5)

### Late Game (Characters 51+ XP)

**Mastering Fame:**
- **All Perks:** Complete fame mastery
- **Fame Level:** International celebrity
- **Total Control:** Media narratives shaped by you
- **Influence Power:** Celebrity used strategically

**Advanced Tactics:**
- Use media to expose supernatural threats (carefully)
- Create public pressure on enemies
- Fame as shield (too visible to disappear)
- Build celebrity Hunter network

**End-game Build:**
- Charisma 5, Manipulation 4-5
- Persuasion 5, Performance 5, Subterfuge 4
- Second/Third Edge: Contacts (synergy), Status (power), Inspire (leadership)
- Public face of Hunter movement

### Edge Synergies

**Works Great With:**
- **Redeemer Creed** - Famous humanitarian Hunter
- **Contacts Edge** - Celebrity + connections = massive network
- **Status Edge** - Fame + authority = total influence
- **Inspire Edge** - Celebrity leader inspiring allies
- **Charisma 5** - Maximum social power

**Conflicts With:**
- **Stealth-based concepts** - Can't hide when famous
- **Low-profile Hunting** - Fame draws attention
- **Avenger (sometimes)** - Hard to seek vengeance publicly

**Neutral:**
- **Arsenal, Library, Fleet** - Different focus but compatible

### Using Fame in Play

Fame operates narratively:

1. **Establish Fame Level** - With Storyteller (local, regional, national, international)
2. **Define Fame Source** - What are you famous for?
3. **Use Perks Narratively** - Describe how fame helps
4. **Roll When Needed** - Social skills with advantage

**Example:**
```
You: "I approach the club entrance. Don't they recognize me?"
Storyteller: "Yeah, Instant Recognition applies. Bouncer's eyes widen."
Bouncer NPC: "Oh wow, you're... please, right this way."
Storyteller: "VIP section, on the house. What are you looking for?"
You: "My contact said they'd be here..."
[Scene proceeds with access gained through fame]
```

**Regional Star:** Known across multi-state area
