# Safehouse üè†

A place of safety. A place of power. A place that's yours.

Safehouse represents a Hunter's secure location - a haven, hideout, base of operations, or fortress. Whether it's an apartment, warehouse, bunker, or mansion, this is your sanctuary where you're safe, equipped, and in control.

---

## Overview

### What is Safehouse?

Safehouse is an **Asset Edge** that grants a secure, defensible location under your control. More than just a home, it's a fortified position with security measures, resources, and tactical advantages for you and your cell.

The Edge operates on **territorial control**: within your Safehouse, you have home advantage. Security systems alert you to threats, defenses protect you, and resources support your operations. It's where you're strongest.

### Who Should Take This Edge?

**Ideal For:**
- **Defenders** - Protect your territory
- **Cell leaders** - Base for team operations
- **Planners** - Need secure planning space
- **Injured Hunters** - Safe recovery location
- **Long-term campaigns** - Permanent base of operations

**Consider Carefully If:**
- Your chronicle is nomadic (always traveling)
- You prefer mobility over fortification
- Storyteller doesn't want fixed locations
- You're a loner who won't share space

### Key Strengths

- **Security** - Alerts and defenses against threats
- **Resources** - Equipment, supplies, medical
- **Team base** - Cell gathering point
- **Recovery** - Safe healing location
- **Tactical advantage** - Home field advantage in fights

### Limitations

- **Fixed location** - Can be found and attacked
- **Maintenance required** - Costs money, time, attention
- **Vulnerable when absent** - Enemies can strike when you're away
- **Shared space** - Cell may have access (less privacy)
- **Storyteller target** - Makes you easier to threaten

## All Perks

### Fortified

**What It Does:**
Your Safehouse has security measures, reinforced structures, and defensive preparations. Intruders trigger alarms, face obstacles, and can be repelled.

**Security Features:**
- **Alarms** - Motion sensors, cameras, tripwires
- **Reinforced doors/windows** - Hard to breach
- **Safe room** - Secure inner sanctum
- **Escape routes** - Hidden exits
- **Defensive positions** - Cover, firing lanes
- **Deterrents** - Lights, barriers, warnings

**Mechanical Effects:**
- Intruders must overcome security (rolls or time)
- You're alerted to breaches automatically
- Advantage defending inside Safehouse
- Difficulty for enemies to breach increases

**When To Use:**
- **Under attack** - Defend your home
- **Hunted** - Retreat to fortress
- **Protecting others** - Shelter witnesses/allies
- **Final stand** - Fortified last stand

**Strategy Notes:**
- Essential for any Safehouse
- Scales with investment (more security = harder to breach)
- Describe specific measures narratively
- Can be upgraded over time

**Example:**
```
Vampire coterie attacks your Safehouse
Fortified activates:
- Perimeter alarms alert you (advance warning)
- Reinforced doors delay entry (time to arm up)
- Camera system shows number and positions
- Safe room available if overwhelmed
You defend from prepared positions with advantage
```

**Herald Commands:**
```
/edge edge:Safehouse
/perks edge:Safehouse perk:Fortified
```

### Armory

**What It Does:**
Your Safehouse contains a well-stocked armory of weapons, ammunition, armor, and tactical equipment. Always have the right tools readily available.

**Armory Contents:**
- **Firearms** - Pistols, rifles, shotguns
- **Melee weapons** - Bats, blades, improvised
- **Ammunition** - Standard and special (silver, blessed)
- **Armor** - Vests, tactical gear
- **Explosives** - Grenades, charges (if legal/available)
- **Tactical equipment** - Night vision, radios, tools

**When To Use:**
- **Mission prep** - Arm up before hunts
- **Emergency response** - Grab weapons quickly
- **Team armament** - Equip entire cell
- **Replacements** - Lost/damaged gear

**Strategy Notes:**
- Different from Arsenal Edge (this is storage, Arsenal is procurement)
- Pairs well with Arsenal (fill Armory with Arsenal-acquired gear)
- Requires security (Fortified prevents theft)
- Storyteller determines what's reasonable

**Example:**
```
Urgent call: Ghoul nest discovered, immediate response needed
Head to Safehouse Armory:
- Grab tactical shotguns (you + 3 allies)
- Load silver shells (effective against supernatural)
- Don tactical vests
- Grab radios and medical kits
Fully equipped in 10 minutes, ready to deploy
```

**Herald Commands:**
```
/perks edge:Safehouse perk:Armory
```

### Infirmary

**What It Does:**
Your Safehouse has medical supplies, equipment, and facilities for treating injuries. Recover from wounds safely and effectively.

**Medical Facilities:**
- **First aid supplies** - Bandages, antiseptic, sutures
- **Medical equipment** - Diagnostic tools, monitors
- **Pharmacy** - Painkillers, antibiotics, specialized meds
- **Surgery capability** - Operating table, tools (if skilled medic available)
- **Recovery beds** - Clean, secure rest area

**Mechanical Effects:**
- Advantage on Medicine rolls for treatment
- Faster healing in Infirmary
- Can treat injuries that would otherwise be untreatable
- Prevent infection and complications

**When To Use:**
- **Post-combat** - Treat injuries after fights
- **Recovery** - Heal between sessions
- **Critical wounds** - Emergency treatment
- **Ally care** - Treat other Hunters

**Strategy Notes:**
- Essential for sustained campaigns
- Pairs with Medicine skill (someone needs to use it)
- Reduces downtime from injuries
- Can hide injured characters from hospitals

**Example:**
```
You took 5 lethal damage in vampire fight (Health 6, badly hurt)
Retreat to Safehouse Infirmary:
- Ally with Medicine 4 treats you
- Infirmary provides advantage: +2 dice
- Roll Medicine to stabilize and begin healing
- Rest safely for days to heal
Survive without hospital visit (which would raise questions)
```

**Herald Commands:**
```
/perks edge:Safehouse perk:Infirmary
```

### Workshop

**What It Does:**
Your Safehouse includes tools, workspace, and equipment for building, repairing, modifying, and crafting gear.

**Workshop Capabilities:**
- **Tool collection** - Comprehensive toolset
- **Workbench** - Space for projects
- **Materials** - Basic supplies, components
- **Specialized equipment** - Depends on focus (electronics, mechanics, woodworking, etc.)

**Applications:**
- **Repair gear** - Fix damaged equipment
- **Modify weapons** - Custom modifications
- **Build equipment** - Create specialized tools
- **Improvise solutions** - MacGyver moments
- **Craft ammunition** - Make special rounds

**When To Use:**
- **Preparation** - Build gear before missions
- **Maintenance** - Keep equipment functional
- **Innovation** - Create custom solutions
- **Between sessions** - Craft projects

**Strategy Notes:**
- Requires Craft skill to use effectively
- Storyteller determines what can be built
- Takes time (not instant)
- Can create unique advantages

**Example:**
```
Need wooden stakes for vampire hunt
Workshop allows:
- Carve stakes from wood supplies
- Make multiple (for whole cell)
- Sharpen properly
- Add hand guards
Ready in 2 hours, no need to buy/find
```

**Herald Commands:**
```
/perks edge:Safehouse perk:Workshop
```

### Archive

**What It Does:**
Your Safehouse contains records, case files, evidence, and documentation of your hunts. Build institutional knowledge over time.

**Archive Contents:**
- **Case files** - Previous hunts documented
- **Evidence** - Photos, artifacts, samples
- **Intelligence** - Threat dossiers
- **Maps** - Territory marked with incidents
- **Contacts** - Relationship records
- **Research notes** - Compiled knowledge

**When To Use:**
- **Pattern identification** - Review past incidents
- **Threat assessment** - Check previous encounters
- **Knowledge preservation** - Record discoveries
- **Team continuity** - New members learn history

**Strategy Notes:**
- Grows more valuable over time
- Essential for long campaigns
- Pairs with Library Edge (books + records)
- Provides historical context

**Example:**
```
New vampire appearance in city
Check Archive:
- Review past vampire incidents (5 years)
- Find pattern: Same feeding grounds
- Check dossiers: Likely same coterie
- Reference old case: Previous defeat method
Use historical knowledge to plan current hunt
```

```
/perks edge:Safehouse perk:Archive
```

## Strategy Guide

### Early Game (Characters 0-20 XP)

**Starting with Safehouse:**
- **Focus:** Basic security and functionality
- **Best Perks:** Fortified, Infirmary
- **Location Type:** Apartment, small house, or rented space
- **Tactics:** Secure base for recovery and planning

**Recommended Build:**
- Invest in Fortified first (security)
- Add Infirmary if anyone has Medicine skill
- Keep location secret from enemies
- Share with cell for team benefit

**Development:**
- Upgrade security over time (better alarms, cameras)
- Stock medical supplies
- Establish clear protocols (who has access, emergency procedures)

### Mid Game (Characters 21-50 XP)

**Developing Safehouse:**
- **Add Perks:** Armory, Workshop, or Archive
- **Upgrade Location:** Better neighborhood, larger space
- **Expand Capabilities:** Multiple functions
- **Team Integration:** Cell headquarters

**Safehouse Evolution:**
- Start: Basic apartment with locks and first aid
- Mid: Warehouse with security, weapons, medical bay
- Advanced: Fortified compound with all facilities

**Progression:**
- Add perks based on cell needs
- Armory if combat-focused
- Archive if investigation-focused
- Workshop if tech/craft-focused

### Late Game (Characters 51+ XP)

**Mastering Safehouse:**
- **All Perks:** Complete fortress
- **Legendary Status:** Known Hunter haven
- **Cell Hub:** Central operations base
- **Tactical Dominance:** Unassailable position

**Advanced Safehouse:**
- Multiple redundant security systems
- Comprehensive armory for any threat
- Full medical facility
- Extensive workshop and archive
- Safe room with supplies for weeks

**End-game Safehouse:**
- Could withstand small army assault
- Fully self-sufficient
- Cell command center
- Legacy location (passed to next generation)

### Edge Synergies

**Works Great With:**
- **Defender Creed** - Protect your territory
- **Arsenal Edge** - Stock armory with procured weapons
- **Library Edge** - Archive + library = complete knowledge base
- **Contacts Edge** - Safe meeting place for informants
- **Cell-based play** - Team benefits from shared base

**Conflicts With:**
- **Nomadic campaigns** - Traveling negates fixed base
- **Loner characters** - Less benefit if solo
- **Avenger (sometimes)** - Vengeance quest may be mobile

**Neutral:**
- **Fame, Fleet, Third Eye** - Different focus but compatible

### Using Safehouse in Play

Safehouse operates narratively and mechanically:

**Narrative:**
- Describe your Safehouse with Storyteller
- Establish location, layout, security
- Define what's inside (based on perks)

**Mechanical:**
- Defending Safehouse: Advantage on rolls
- Using facilities: Advantage on relevant skill checks
- Security: Alerts and impediments for intruders

**Example:**
```
Storyteller: "The vampires tracked you. They're outside your Safehouse."
You: "I'm alerted by perimeter sensors (Fortified). How many?"
Storyteller: "Four vampires, approaching the door."
You: "I grab weapons from the Armory, take position behind fortified cover."
Storyteller: "They breach the outer door - difficulty increased by Fortified. Roll initiative."
You fight with home advantage: +2 dice for defending your Safehouse
```
