Authority. Position. Power. You have all three.

Status represents a Hunter's institutional authority and official position. Whether you're a police detective, government official, military officer, corporate executive, or religious authority, you have legitimate power that others must respect.

---

## Overview

### What is Status?

Status is an **Asset Edge** that grants authority within an institution or organization. You have a position of power that comes with privileges, access, resources, and the ability to give orders that must be obeyed.

The Edge operates on **institutional authority**: you're not just connected (like Contacts) or famous (like Fame) - you have actual legal/organizational power. You can issue commands, access resources, and exercise authority backed by your institution.

### Who Should Take This Edge?

**Ideal For:**
- **Authority figures** - Police, military, government
- **Defenders** - Protect through institutional power
- **Zealots** - Lead crusades with authority
- **Investigators** - Official access and resources
- **Leaders** - Command and direct others

**Consider Carefully If:**
- Your character concept is anti-authority
- Your chronicle doesn't involve institutions
- You prefer operating outside the system
- Storyteller doesn't want official characters

### Key Strengths

- **Legal authority** - Power backed by law/organization
- **Resource access** - Official budgets and equipment
- **Information access** - Classified/restricted data
- **Command structure** - Give orders that are obeyed
- **Protection** - Institutional backing and support

### Limitations

- **Accountability** - Must answer to superiors
- **Bureaucracy** - Red tape and regulations
- **Public scrutiny** - Actions are recorded and reviewed
- **Chain of command** - Can't act completely independently
- **Institutional constraints** - Bound by organizational rules

## All Perks

### Official Resources

**What It Does:**
You have access to your institution's resources, budget, equipment, and facilities. Use organizational assets for your purposes (within limits).

**Available Resources:**
- **Budget** - Official funds for operations
- **Equipment** - Institutional gear and tools
- **Facilities** - Buildings, labs, offices
- **Personnel** - Staff and subordinates
- **Vehicles** - Official transportation
- **Information systems** - Databases, records

**Examples by Institution:**
- **Police:** Squad cars, forensic labs, databases, officers
- **Military:** Weapons, vehicles, bases, soldiers
- **Government:** Offices, budgets, staff, clearances
- **Corporate:** Money, lawyers, facilities, employees
- **Religious:** Buildings, funds, congregation, authority

**When To Use:**
- **Mission support** - Use official resources
- **Investigations** - Access records and databases
- **Operations** - Requisition equipment
- **Cover** - Official business justification

**Strategy Notes:**
- Powerful but requires justification
- Abuse draws investigation
- Coordinate with Storyteller on limits
- Some uses require approval rolls

**Example:**
```
Need to investigate crime scene
Status: Police Detective
Official Resources: Access police database, forensics lab, squad car
Roll: Manipulation + relevant skill to justify use
Success: Full access, no questions asked
Use institutional resources for Hunt
```

### Chain of Command

**What It Does:**
You can give orders to subordinates within your organization, and they must obey (barring illegal/immoral commands). Command others to assist your operations.

**Command Capabilities:**
- **Direct subordinates** - Those under you
- **Request cooperation** - Peers and parallel units
- **Invoke authority** - Use rank/position
- **Assign tasks** - Delegate work
- **Requisition support** - Call for backup

**When To Use:**
- **Investigations** - Assign officers/staff to research
- **Operations** - Deploy teams
- **Security** - Order protection
- **Information gathering** - Delegate inquiries

**Strategy Notes:**
- Limited to your institution
- Subordinates may question unusual orders
- Abuse damages effectiveness
- Works best for legitimate-seeming tasks

**Example:**
```
Need surveillance on suspected vampire haven
Status: Police Lieutenant
Chain of Command: Order patrol increase in area
- Officers report suspicious activity
- Surveillance established legitimately
- Information flows to you
Hunt benefits from official operations
```

**Herald Commands:**
```
/perks edge:Status perk:Chain of Command
```

### Jurisdictional Authority

**What It Does:**
Your authority extends across your institution's jurisdiction. Access areas, question people, and exercise power throughout your domain.

**Authority Scope:**
- **Geographic** - Your jurisdiction's territory
- **Functional** - Your department's purview
- **Legal** - What your position allows
- **Practical** - What you can get away with

**Applications:**
- **Access control** - Enter restricted areas
- **Questioning** - Interview people officially
- **Searches** - Examine premises (with justification)
- **Detainment** - Temporarily hold suspects
- **Evidence collection** - Gather materials

**When To Use:**
- **Investigation access** - Get into places legally
- **Interviews** - Official questioning
- **Evidence gathering** - Collect materials
- **Territorial control** - Exercise authority

**Strategy Notes:**
- Requires legitimate justification
- Overreach invites trouble
- Different institutions have different jurisdictions
- Supernatural threats often outside normal jurisdiction

**Example:**
```
Vampire operating from nightclub
Status: Fire Inspector
Jurisdictional Authority: Enter and inspect building
- Legal access without warrant
- Examine all areas (looking for violations)
- Document layout and security
- Identify vampire's lair location
Hunt planning benefits from official access
```

```
/perks edge:Status perk:Jurisdictional Authority
```

### Institutional Protection

**What It Does:**
Your institution provides legal protection, cover, and support when you face consequences. Lawyers, PR teams, and organizational backing shield you.

**Protection Types:**
- **Legal defense** - Lawyers and legal teams
- **PR management** - Spin and damage control
- **Plausible deniability** - Official cover stories
- **Investigation interference** - Slow/stop inquiries
- **Resource defense** - Financial/political support

**When To Use:**
- **Legal trouble** - Arrests, investigations
- **Public incidents** - Media attention
- **Political pressure** - External threats
- **Internal review** - Organizational inquiry

**Strategy Notes:**
- Doesn't make you immune (just protected)
- Serious violations still have consequences
- Effectiveness varies by institution power
- Must maintain good standing

**Example:**
```
Shootout with ghouls in public park
Police respond, you're detained
Institutional Protection (Military):
- Military lawyers arrive
- "Classified training exercise" cover story
- Civilian authorities defer to military
- You're released with apology
Hunt incident covered by institution
```

**Herald Commands:**
```
/perks edge:Status perk:Institutional Protection
```

### Political Clout

**What It Does:**
Your position grants political influence beyond your direct authority. Leverage your status to affect policy, bend rules, and influence decisions.

**Influence Applications:**
- **Policy shaping** - Affect institutional direction
- **Rule bending** - Get exceptions approved
- **Resource allocation** - Direct budgets/personnel
- **Interdepartmental cooperation** - Cross-agency work
- **External influence** - Affect other institutions

**When To Use:**
- **Strategic operations** - Large-scale hunts
- **Inter-agency coordination** - Multiple institutions
- **Policy protection** - Prevent harmful regulations
- **Long-term planning** - Shape institutional response

**Strategy Notes:**
- Most powerful Status perk
- Requires high position in institution
- Limited uses (political capital finite)
- Long-term effects on chronicle

**Example:**
```
City government passing law restricting surveillance
Would harm Hunter operations
Political Clout (City Council Member):
- Lobby against law
- Propose amendments
- Rally political support
- Law modified to preserve Hunter capabilities
Long-term strategic victory
```

**Herald Commands:**
```
/perks edge:Status perk:Political Clout
```

## Strategy Guide

### Early Game (Characters 0-20 XP)

**Starting with Status:**
- **Focus:** Basic authority utilization
- **Best Perks:** Official Resources, Jurisdictional Authority
- **Position Level:** Entry-level authority (detective, lieutenant, junior official)
- **Tactics:** Use legitimate access for Hunt

**Recommended Build:**
- Manipulation 3-4 (justify actions)
- Relevant professional skills (varies by institution)
- Official Resources first (immediate utility)
- Maintain cover as legitimate professional

**Challenges:**
- Balancing official duties and Hunt
- Hiding supernatural knowledge from institution
- Avoiding suspicion from superiors

### Mid Game (Characters 21-50 XP)

**Developing Status:**
- **Add Perks:** Chain of Command, Institutional Protection
- **Position Growth:** Mid-level authority (captain, major, senior official)
- **Expand Influence:** Broader jurisdiction and resources
- **Strategic Use:** Coordinate official and Hunter operations

**Authority Evolution:**
- Start: Use position for access and information
- Mid: Command resources and personnel
- Advanced: Shape institutional response to threats

**Progression:**
- Raise Manipulation (3-5) for authority use
- Professional skills relevant to position
- Second Edge: Contacts (official + informal networks), Arsenal (equip through official channels)

### Late Game (Characters 51+ XP)

**Mastering Status:**
- **All Perks:** Complete institutional power
- **High Position:** Chief, general, director, senior official
- **Political Power:** Affect policy and strategy
- **Institutional Hunter:** Lead official Hunt efforts

**Advanced Tactics:**
- Use Political Clout for long-term strategy
- Create official (but secret) Hunter units
- Protect other Hunters through institutional power
- Shape societal response to supernatural

**End-game Status:**
- Top of institutional hierarchy
- Massive resources available
- Can deploy official forces against threats
- Potential conflict: institution vs Hunt priorities

### Edge Synergies

**Works Great With:**
- **Defender Creed** - Official protector
- **Zealot Creed** - Institutional crusader
- **Contacts Edge** - Official + informal = total network
- **Fame Edge** - Authority + celebrity = maximum influence
- **Arsenal Edge** - Official armories

**Conflicts With:**
- **Criminal activities** - Harder to justify as official
- **Anti-authority concepts** - Thematic clash
- **Avenger (sometimes)** - Vengeance vs procedure

**Neutral:**
- **Library, Fleet, Third Eye** - Different focus but compatible

### Using Status in Play

Status operates through justified authority:

1. **Establish institution** - What organization? What position?
2. **Identify need** - What do you want to do?
3. **Justify officially** - How does this fit your job?
4. **Roll if needed** - Manipulation + relevant skill
5. **Exercise authority** - Use institutional power

**Example:**
```
You: "I need to access the city's surveillance cameras near the vampire haven"
Storyteller: "You're a police detective. How do you justify this?"
You: "Investigating suspicious activity reports in the area - official police business"
Storyteller: "Okay, roll Manipulation + Investigation to get access"
/roll pool:7 difficulty:2 comment:Official access request
Success: IT department grants access, no questions
You review footage for vampire activity
```
