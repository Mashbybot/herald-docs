Library represents a Hunter's access to books, archives, databases, and information resources. Whether you maintain a personal collection, have access to institutional archives, or possess rare occult texts, you can research anything given time and effort.

## Overview

### What is Library?

Library is an **Asset Edge** that grants access to comprehensive information resources for research, study, and knowledge acquisition. You have books, documents, databases, and archives covering history, occult lore, supernatural threats, and esoteric knowledge.

The Edge operates on **research capability**: given time, you can find information on almost any topic. Unlike Contacts (asking people) or Global Access (digital networks), Library is about **traditional research** through texts, documents, and accumulated knowledge.

### Who Should Take This Edge?

**Ideal For:**
- **Investigators** - Intelligence/Investigation focused
- **Innocents** - Learn through study and research
- **Occult specialists** - Supernatural knowledge seekers
- **Support characters** - Provide intel to combat teams
- **Strategic planners** - Knowledge-based preparation

**Consider Carefully If:**
- Your character is uneducated or anti-intellectual
- Your chronicle is action-focused with no research time
- Storyteller doesn't enjoy research scenes
- You prefer street knowledge to book learning

### Key Strengths

- **Comprehensive knowledge** - Research any topic
- **Occult lore** - Supernatural threat information
- **Historical records** - Past events and patterns
- **Preparation advantage** - Know enemy weaknesses
- **Self-sufficient** - Don't need contacts or external help

### Limitations

- **Time-intensive** - Research takes hours to days
- **Requires Intelligence** - Low Int limits effectiveness
- **Stationary** - Must access library physically (usually)
- **Interpretation needed** - Books don't always have clear answers
- **Storyteller-dependent** - Information availability varies

## All Perks

### Comprehensive Collection

**What It Does:**
Your library covers an extremely wide range of topics with depth. You have resources on history, science, occult, mythology, supernatural creatures, and esoteric knowledge.

**Coverage Areas:**
- **History** - Local, national, world history
- **Occult** - Supernatural lore, magic, rituals
- **Mythology** - Cultural myths, legends, folklore
- **Bestiary** - Creature information (vampires, werewolves, ghosts, etc.)
- **Science** - Academic texts, research papers
- **Religion** - Religious texts, theology, comparative religion
- **Rare Texts** - Out-of-print, ancient, forbidden knowledge

**When To Use:**
- **Threat identification** - What is this creature?
- **Historical research** - What happened here before?
- **Ritual information** - How does this magic work?
- **Weakness discovery** - How do we kill it?

**Strategy Notes:**
- Foundation for all other Library perks
- Broader collection = more topics available
- Storyteller determines what's in your library
- Build collection over time in-game

**Example:**
```
Encountering strange creature - humanoid, pale, drinks blood
Research in Comprehensive Collection
Topic: Vampires
Find: Multiple texts on vampire lore across cultures
Learn: Weaknesses (sunlight, stakes, fire), powers (strength, mesmerism), habits (need blood, avoid running water)
```

### Obscure Lore

**What It Does:**
Your library contains rare, forbidden, or lost knowledge that few others can access. Obscure texts, ancient languages, suppressed information, and dangerous knowledge unavailable elsewhere.

**Examples:**
- **Forbidden texts** - Banned or destroyed knowledge
- **Ancient languages** - Texts requiring translation
- **Secret histories** - Suppressed truths
- **Lost sciences** - Forgotten techniques
- **Elder lore** - Knowledge of ancient beings
- **Personal notes** - Previous Hunters' journals

**When To Use:**
- **Unique threats** - Standard resources fail
- **Deep mysteries** - Uncover hidden truths
- **Legendary enemies** - Research ancient foes
- **Forbidden knowledge** - Learn dangerous secrets

**Strategy Notes:**

- Obscure Lore can reveal anything Storyteller allows

### Cross-Reference

**What It Does:**
You excel at finding connections between disparate sources, identifying patterns across multiple texts, and synthesizing information from various fields to reach insights.

**Mechanical Effect:**
- Combine information from multiple sources
- Find patterns others miss
- Connect seemingly unrelated facts
- Bonus successes on research rolls with margin

**When To Use:**
- **Complex investigations** - Multiple clues
- **Pattern identification** - Serial incidents
- **Conspiracy unraveling** - Connect the dots
- **Historical parallels** - "This happened before"

**Strategy Notes:**
- Multiplies effectiveness of Comprehensive Collection
- Best with high Intelligence (analyzing connections)
- Pairs with Investigation skill
- Can reveal hidden plots

**Example:**
```
Researching recent disappearances in city
Cross-Reference across:
- Local history books (similar disappearances 50 years ago)
- Occult texts (feeding patterns match vampire behavior)
- Census records (victims all lived near old mansion)
- Property records (mansion owned by "M. Vane" for 150 years, different first names)
Conclusion: Vampire elder, same entity across time, operating from mansion
```

## Strategy Guide

### Early Game (Characters 0-20 XP)

**Starting with Library:**
- **Focus:** Basic research capability
- **Best Perks:** Comprehensive Collection, Rapid Research
- **Library Scope:** Personal collection or institutional access
- **Tactics:** Research threats before engaging

**Recommended Build:**
- Intelligence 4, Wits 3
- Investigation 4, Occult 3, Academics 2
- Comprehensive Collection first (foundation)
- Rapid Research second (playability)

**Research Routine:**
1. Encounter threat
2. Gather initial information
3. Research in library (Rapid Research for speed)
4. Learn weaknesses and tactics
5. Prepare and engage

### Mid Game (Characters 21-50 XP)

**Developing Library:**
- **Add Perks:** Ritual Knowledge, Cross-Reference
- **Expand Collection:** Acquire rare texts in-game
- **Specialization:** Focus on common threat types
- **Integration:** Combine with field investigations

**Research Strategy:**
- Maintain threat dossiers (vampire families, werewolf packs, etc.)
- Build pattern databases (ritual sites, attack patterns)
- Cross-reference new incidents with historical data
- Share knowledge with cell

**Progression:**
- Raise Investigation to 5 (maximum research effectiveness)
- Raise Occult to 4-5 (understand supernatural)
- Buy second Edge (Contacts for human sources, Third Eye for supernatural sight)

### Late Game (Characters 51+ XP)

**Mastering Library:**
- **All Perks:** Complete library mastery
- **Comprehensive Collection:** Legendary repository
- **Expert Researcher:** Find anything given time
- **Knowledge Authority:** Cell's information source

**Advanced Tactics:**
- Obscure Lore for impossible questions
- Cross-Reference for conspiracy unraveling
- Ritual Knowledge for magical solutions
- Rapid Research for instant answers

**End-game Build:**
- Intelligence 5, Investigation 5, Occult 5
- Second/Third Edge: Contacts (total information), Global Access (digital + physical)
- Academics 4-5, multiple knowledge specialties
- Master researcher and lore keeper

### Edge Synergies

**Works Great With:**
- **Innocent Creed** - Learn through study
- **Contacts Edge** - Human + textual sources = comprehensive intel
- **Global Access Edge** - Digital + physical research
- **Third Eye Edge** - See truth, verify with research
- **High Intelligence** - Maximum analytical capability

**Conflicts With:**
- **Low Intelligence characters** - Can't use effectively
- **Action-only chronicles** - No time for research
- **Anti-intellectual concepts** - Doesn't fit character

**Neutral:**
- **Arsenal, Fleet, Martyr** - Different focus but compatible

### Using Library in Play

Library operates through research rolls:

1. **Identify research topic** - What do you want to know?
2. **Storyteller sets difficulty** - Based on obscurity
3. **Apply perks** - Rapid Research (time), Obscure Lore (availability)
4. **Roll Intelligence + Investigation or Occult**
5. **Receive information** - Based on successes

**Example:**
```
You: "I want to research this vampire's history - Marcus Vane"
Storyteller: "Okay, Intelligence + Investigation, Difficulty 3 (not common knowledge)"
You have Rapid Research (2 hours instead of 2 days)
/roll pool:9 difficulty:3 comment:Researching Marcus Vane
(Intelligence 5 + Investigation 4 = 9 dice)
Result: 6 successes, Margin 3
Storyteller provides detailed dossier based on margin
```
